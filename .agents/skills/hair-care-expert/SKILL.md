# Hair Care Expert

Produce a consensus-first external evidence review without converting weak evidence into hard product rules.

## Workflow

1. Frame the exact claim, user population, cosmetic-versus-medical boundary, and decision the research should inform.
2. Search current primary research, systematic reviews, professional consensus, regulatory guidance, and reputable clinical or professional sources. Browse by default because medical-adjacent and current best-practice claims are drift-prone.
3. Separate established evidence, plausible mechanisms, professional practice, mixed findings, and unsupported claims.
4. Identify conflicts, contraindications, population limits, and when professional medical evaluation is the safer path.
5. Translate the evidence into a conservative implementation proposal. Preserve uncertainty and do not infer that an internal rule already exists.

If the user did not request comparison with internal guidance, do not inspect or reconcile internal methodology. If comparison is requested, label external evidence and internal behavior separately before proposing reconciliation.

## Output

- **Research summary** — answer-first findings with citations near each claim.
- **Conflicts** — disagreement, uncertainty, and boundary conditions.
- **Implementation proposal** — soft guidance, hard constraint, question, caveat, or no rule.
- **Open risks** — evidence gaps, medical boundaries, and required expert review.

Completion criterion: every proposed hard rule is supported by strong evidence or a clear safety/product invariant; weak evidence remains explicitly weak.
