A is too small

It improves today's pain but keeps the core problem: local files and invisible work. It is not enough for a scalable intake process.

B is the best next step

It gives Nick a real queue, durable job state, scored research, visible rework, and one explicit publishing handoff without jumping to risky auto-publish.

C comes later

Auto-publish is attractive, but only after the cockpit produces enough audit history to prove the research and image gates are reliable.
