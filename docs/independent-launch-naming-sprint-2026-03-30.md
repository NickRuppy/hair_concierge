# Independent Launch Naming Sprint

Date: 2026-03-30

## Context

The product currently appears in the codebase and existing docs as a legacy advisor-branded experience, but launch naming now needs to be treated as a fresh decision.

This sprint assumes:

- no advisor-linked naming for launch
- no adjacency to legacy brand families unless that is later confirmed safe
- screening is intentionally deferred until a clear favorite emerges
- the core name should stay suggestive rather than literal

This document is creative direction only. It does not rename product copy in the app yet.

## Naming Brief

### What the name must do

- feel expert-luxe rather than gimmicky, clinical, or trend-chasing
- work in German and English without sounding awkward
- feel gender-neutral on first impression
- leave room for the product to grow beyond quiz + chat into scanner, routine tracking, and broader personalized care
- sound like a real brand, not a feature label

### What to avoid

- `bot`, `AI`, `lab`, `clinic`, `genius`, or similar category cliches
- literal `Hair` or `Haar` names in the core mark unless a standout exception appears later
- names that depend on a specific advisor's personal authority to make sense
- names that feel too narrow for a future platform

### Naming lens for this round

- distinctive over descriptive
- easy to say over clever on paper
- one clear emotional signal over mixed metaphors
- ownable mood over feature explanation

## Finalist Shortlist

These are the strongest options based on immediate brand fit only. Screening, domains, handles, and trademark work are intentionally postponed.

### 1. `Avenor` - Lead Candidate

- Rationale: Best balance of trust, premium tone, neutrality, and long-term brand stretch.
- Pronunciation: `AH-veh-nor` in German, `AV-eh-nor` in English.
- Emotional signal: Calm authority.
- Scale potential: Easily extends from advisor to scanner, tracking, product matching, and content.

### 2. `Velune`

- Rationale: Strongest beauty-led option without becoming soft, generic, or overtly feminine.
- Pronunciation: `veh-LOON` in German, `veh-LOON` or `veh-LUNE` in English.
- Emotional signal: Polish and elevated care.
- Scale potential: Could stretch into product experiences, premium content, and result-card branding.

### 3. `Valence`

- Rationale: Most intelligent-feeling option; credible, balanced, and serious without sounding medical.
- Pronunciation: `VA-lenz` in German, `VAY-lens` in English.
- Emotional signal: Precision and confidence.
- Scale potential: Strong fit for a broader platform with diagnosis, scoring, compatibility, and routines.

### 4. `Lucera`

- Rationale: Brings clarity and beauty together in a way that feels premium and consumer-friendly.
- Pronunciation: `loo-TSE-ra` in German, `loo-SAIR-uh` or `loo-SEH-rah` in English.
- Emotional signal: Light, clarity, visible improvement.
- Scale potential: Works for a brand that wants to feel more luminous and consumer beauty-oriented.

### 5. `North Edit`

- Rationale: Strong editorial route; instantly signals direction, curation, and premium taste.
- Pronunciation: `north e-dit` in both German and English.
- Emotional signal: Curated confidence.
- Scale potential: Could work well if the brand wants to feel more lifestyle and editorial than beauty-tech.

### 6. `Verune`

- Rationale: Good hybrid between evidence, softness, and premium mood; more ownable-feeling than many real words.
- Pronunciation: `veh-ROO-neh` in German, `veh-ROON` or `veh-RUNE` in English.
- Emotional signal: Quiet sophistication.
- Scale potential: Broad enough for a platform and soft enough for consumer beauty.

### 7. `Veyra`

- Rationale: Most fashion-forward finalist; punchy, modern, and easy to imagine in a premium identity system.
- Pronunciation: `VAY-ra` in German and English.
- Emotional signal: Modern confidence.
- Scale potential: Best if the brand wants a sharper, cooler public image.

## Recommendation

### Recommended winner: `Avenor`

`Avenor` is the strongest first pick because it sounds like an actual premium brand, stays independent from advisor-led legacy branding, and has enough gravity to carry a broader platform story later. It does not overpromise one feature, and it leaves room for the product to evolve without forcing a rename.

### Strong alternates

- `Velune` if the brand should lean more beauty-first and emotionally polished
- `Valence` if the brand should lean more intelligence, rigor, and compatibility-scoring
- `Lucera` if the brand should feel brighter, more consumer-facing, and visibly transformational

## Descriptor Formats

These are not slogans. They are clean launch descriptors that can sit under the brand name.

- `Avenor - personalisierte Haarpflege-Beratung`
- `Avenor - Analyse, Routine und Produktempfehlungen fuer dein Haar`
- `Avenor - dein personalisierter Haarpflege-Kompass`

React to the shortlist first, then take the best 2-3 names into screening. Until a name clearly hits, there is no value in doing domain or legal work across the full candidate pool.
