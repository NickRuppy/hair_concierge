# Oily Scalp

Use when:
- `scalp_type = oily`
- `concerns` includes oily_scalp
- or the user mentions greasy roots, oily scalp, hair getting oily quickly, needing frequent washing, or flat roots from oil

Advisor interpretation:
- Roots and scalp area usually need lighter choices and clearer separation from length care.
- Washing frequency should match the scalp and lifestyle; do not imply the user must train the scalp to need less washing.
- Rich products too close to roots often worsen the feel.
- Over-treating the scalp can create more irritation; keep reset language proportional.

Category implications:
- Shampoo: Keep the main lever scalp-focused cleansing; clarify/reset only when residue or coating signals support it.
- Conditioner: Apply mainly to mid-lengths and ends; avoid root-heavy placement unless product data explicitly supports scalp use.
- Leave-in: Keep lightweight and away from roots; use only where lengths need slip, heat protection, or frizz control.
- Mask: Length-only and occasional; rich masks near roots can make oiliness or flatness feel worse.
- Oil: Avoid scalp/root oiling as default; if used, keep a tiny amount on dry ends only.
- Bondbuilder / repair: Separate damage logic from oiliness; avoid root-heavy application unless the product role requires and supports it.

Routine implications:
- Build around scalp cleansing plus targeted length care, not all-over richness.
- Dry shampoo can be framed as temporary oil management, not scalp cleansing, and should not replace washing indefinitely.

Avoid:
- Do not tell the user to wash less as a universal fix.
- Do not add oils or rich leave-ins near the scalp.
- Do not treat oily scalp and dandruff as the same unless flakes/dandruff are explicit.
- Do not escalate to harsh resets without buildup signals.

Ask only if:
- it is unclear whether the user means oily roots, coated lengths, dandruff/flakes, or irritation, and that would change the answer.

Proactive next step:
- Offer a placement rule that separates scalp cleansing from length conditioning.
