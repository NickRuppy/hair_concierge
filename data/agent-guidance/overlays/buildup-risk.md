# Buildup Risk

Use when:
- the user reports coated, waxy, heavy, greasy, limp, sticky, or unresponsive hair
- or profile/routine context suggests heavy product layering, frequent dry shampoo, regular oiling, CWC/OWC, co-washing, infrequent cleansing, or repeated styling-product residue

Advisor interpretation:
- Treat residue and over-layering as plausible before recommending richer care.
- Buildup can mimic dryness, frizz, flatness, rough feel, or lack of definition, especially on fine or textured hair.
- Separate scalp buildup, oily roots, and fibre coating when possible; they can need different wording.
- Reset language should stay proportional and occasional, not punitive.

Category implications:
- Shampoo: Consider a proportional clarifying or reset frame when residue is central; keep routine shampoo scalp-focused and not harsh by default.
- Conditioner: Keep placement on mid-lengths and ends; avoid escalating richness while hair feels coated or heavy.
- Leave-in: Prefer smaller amounts, lighter textures, or temporary reduction before adding another layer.
- Mask: Pause or stretch heavy masks if hair is soft, flat, coated, or slow to dry; use only occasional lighter length care when still needed.
- Oil: High caution; reduce, skip, or keep only a tiny end-focused amount when coating or limpness is the problem.
- Bondbuilder / repair: Do not use repair language to solve film; consider it only when damage signals are separate and credible.

Routine implications:
- Simplify first: remove redundant leave-in/oil/mask layers, clarify/reset if appropriate, then rebuild only the steps that still solve a visible need.
- For curly or coily hair, preserve slip and definition support while reducing excess film.

Avoid:
- Do not mention hard water or chelating from this overlay alone.
- Do not turn every limp or oily case into a harsh reset.
- Do not add oil, mask, or leave-in complexity when the likely problem is too much film.
- Do not frame buildup as poor hygiene.

Ask only if:
- it is unclear whether the problem feels dry/rough versus coated/heavy, and that distinction would change the advice.

Proactive next step:
- Offer a short reset-and-rebuild plan or a layer audit of the current routine.
