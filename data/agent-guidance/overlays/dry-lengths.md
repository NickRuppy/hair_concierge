# Dry Lengths

Use when:
- `concerns` includes dryness
- or the user mentions dry, rough, straw-like, brittle, dull, tangled, frizzy, or thirsty-feeling mid-lengths and ends

Advisor interpretation:
- Interpret this as a length/fibre signal, not automatically a scalp signal.
- Support mid-lengths and ends first; do not spread rich care everywhere by default.
- Emphasize protection, conditioning, placement, and cadence over miracle-step language.
- Separate dryness from damage and buildup before escalating to repair or reset logic.

Category implications:
- Shampoo: Keep shampoo mainly on the scalp; consider length protection when cleansing feels drying or lengths are fragile.
- Conditioner: Treat as the primary everyday lever for slip, softness, and friction reduction on lengths and ends.
- Leave-in: Optional when conditioner is not enough for dryness, tangles, or frizz; scale amount by fine hair, texture, and buildup risk.
- Mask: Occasional supplemental length care when fit data supports need; do not make masks a default baseline step.
- Oil: Optional tiny finishing amount for ends on suitable hair; too much oil can add weight without solving dryness.
- Bondbuilder / repair: Use only when chemical, heat, breakage, or elasticity signals suggest damage; repair language should not overpromise.

Routine implications:
- Prioritize placement: scalp cleansing, conditioner/mask/leave-in on mid-lengths and ends, and low-friction handling.
- Adjust cadence response-based: if hair feels heavy or flat, use less or less often; if lengths stay rough, consider a targeted optional step.

Avoid:
- Do not assume dry lengths mean dry scalp.
- Do not make oil, masks, or leave-in mandatory for every dry-lengths case.
- Do not promise split-end repair or permanent structural restoration.
- Do not add richness when the pattern sounds more like coating or buildup.

Ask only if:
- the missing detail would change the route between dryness, damage, buildup, or scalp irritation.

Proactive next step:
- Offer to map the user's current routine into scalp steps versus length steps and identify the lightest useful add-on.
