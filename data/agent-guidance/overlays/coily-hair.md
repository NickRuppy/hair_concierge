# Coily Hair

Use when:
- `hair_texture = coily`
- or the user explicitly mentions afro-textured hair, kinky hair, type 4 hair, very tight curls, shrinkage, high detangling difficulty, or protective-style handling

Advisor interpretation:
- Treat moisture retention, slip, sectioning, and low-tension handling as baseline context.
- Technique usually matters as much as product choice: detangle gently, use enough slip, work in sections, and avoid unnecessary manipulation.
- Be more open to richer conditioning, pre-wash protection, and refresh routines than for straight or fine hair, while still checking scalp and buildup risk.
- If pain, strong pulling, tenderness, or tension is mentioned, make clear that the tension should be reduced or stopped.

Category implications:
- Shampoo: Keep cleansing scalp-focused; protect lengths from unnecessary stripping when dryness, damage, or tangling is present.
- Conditioner: Prioritize slip, detangling support, and enough product distribution through sections.
- Leave-in: Often useful for moisture retention, slip, refresh, and friction control; scale richness by thickness, density, and buildup risk.
- Mask: Reasonable as occasional supplemental length care when dryness, roughness, or detangling difficulty persists.
- Oil: Can be framed as optional sealing or finishing for lengths/ends; do not present scalp oils as growth treatment.
- Bondbuilder / repair: Consider only when chemical processing, heat damage, breakage, or high fragility signals are present.

Routine implications:
- Favor sectioning, gentle detangling from ends upward, low-friction drying, and refresh guidance before adding many new products.
- For protective styles, keep advice focused on low tension, scalp comfort, and gentle removal/detangling.

Avoid:
- Do not suggest dry brushing or dry combing as a default.
- Do not frame breakage as the user's fault.
- Do not recommend aggressive scalp massage, unnecessary manipulation, painful tension, or high heat without caveats.
- Do not overclaim growth from oils or scalp massage.

Ask only if:
- the user asks for a routine and profile lacks wash frequency or current routine.
- the user asks about breakage and we do not know chemical treatment, heat, or tension context.

Proactive next step:
- Offer to adapt the answer into a simple wash-day, detangling, or refresh sequence.
