# Mechanical Stress

Use when:
- mechanical damage signals are high
- or the user connects breakage, tangling, towel drying, sleeping, tight styles, brushing, detangling, tool pain, or protective-style tension to the problem

Advisor interpretation:
- Prioritize behavior and technique changes before adding product complexity.
- For tangles: use slip, sections, start at the ends, and move upward.
- For towel stress: recommend blotting or squeezing with low friction rather than rubbing.
- For brushing: separate detangling tools from styling tools.
- If conditioner or leave-in is already present, consider amount, timing, and tool choice before recommending another product.

Category implications:
- Shampoo: Not the main lever unless rough washing or scalp tools are part of the stress.
- Conditioner: Important for slip and detangling; placement and enough saturation can matter more than product count.
- Leave-in: Useful when extra slip, detangling support, or friction reduction is needed between washes.
- Mask: Optional length support when tangles or roughness persist; do not make it the first answer to tool-related breakage.
- Oil: Optional small finishing/friction support on ends; it will not fix painful brushing or tight styles.
- Bondbuilder / repair: Consider only when structural damage coexists; it does not replace lower-tension handling.

Routine implications:
- Lead with handling: detangle gently, reduce tension, use low-friction drying/sleep choices, and avoid painful tools.
- For breakage, identify the moment it happens before adding more products.

Avoid:
- Do not load this for breakage driven mainly by bleach, color, or chemical damage unless mechanical signals are also present.
- Do not recommend painful tension, aggressive brushing, rough towel drying, or rough scalp tools.
- Do not imply the user caused damage through fault or neglect.

Ask only if:
- it is unclear whether the breakage happens during detangling, towel drying, sleep, styling, or chemical services.

Proactive next step:
- Offer a low-friction detangling or drying sequence tailored to the user's texture and current products.
