# Mechanical Stress

Use when:
- mechanical damage signals are high
- or the user connects breakage, tangling, towel drying, sleeping, tight styles, brushing, detangling, or tool pain to the problem

Reasoning guidance:
- Prioritize behavior and technique changes before adding product complexity.
- For tangles: use slip, sections, start at the ends, and move upward.
- For towel stress: recommend blotting or squeezing with low friction rather than rubbing.
- For brushing: separate detangling tools from styling tools.
- If conditioner or leave-in is already present, consider amount, timing, and tool choice before recommending another product.

Avoid:
- Do not load this for breakage driven mainly by bleach, color, or chemical damage unless mechanical signals are also present.
- Do not recommend painful tension, aggressive brushing, or rough scalp tools.

Ask only if:
- it is unclear whether the breakage happens during detangling, towel drying, sleep, styling, or chemical services.
