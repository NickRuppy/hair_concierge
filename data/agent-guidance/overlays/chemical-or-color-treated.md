# Chemical Or Color Treated

Use when:
- `chemical_treatment` includes `colored` or `bleached`, or the user mentions highlights, bleach, dye, color-treated hair, relaxer, perm, keratin/smoothing service, or recent chemical processing.

Advisor interpretation:
- Treat chemical processing as a fragility and porosity context, with bleach usually the stronger damage signal.
- Separate three user jobs: color maintenance, damage stabilization, and dryness/manageability support.
- Product color-protection claims must come from supported product data; do not infer them from names, descriptions, or generic category fit.
- For scalp burning, rash, swelling, or strong irritation after a chemical service, keep advice conservative and point away from cosmetic troubleshooting.

Category implications:
- Shampoo: Use gentle/proportional cleansing language; color-safe or color-protection claims require explicit supported data.
- Conditioner: Important for slip, friction reduction, and length manageability after chemical services.
- Leave-in: Useful for heat protection, detangling, smoothing, or length protection when those roles are supported.
- Mask: Optional supplemental length care when dryness, roughness, porosity, or damage is present; do not make it a scalp treatment.
- Oil: Optional finish or pre-wash length support where appropriate; do not call it color protection or structural repair.
- Bondbuilder / repair: Often relevant for bleach, highlights, relaxer, perm, or repeated chemical stress; explain the lane without promising virgin-hair restoration.

Routine implications:
- Reduce compounding stress: heat, rough detangling, tight styling, and too-frequent chemical services can outrun product benefits.
- When color maintenance is the user's main goal, mention that product data determines whether color-safe guidance is supported.
- When damage is the main goal, route toward repair/protection and gentle handling rather than color claims.

Avoid:
- Do not claim a product protects color unless the selected-product data says so.
- Do not promise reversal of bleach/color/chemical damage.
- Do not make sulfate-free or any single ingredient rule a hard requirement from this overlay alone.
- Do not ignore scalp symptoms after dye, bleach, relaxer, perm, or smoothing services.

Ask only if:
- the type, timing, or severity of the chemical service would materially change whether the answer is about color maintenance, repair, moisture, or scalp caution.

Proactive next step:
- Offer to choose the right lane first: color maintenance, damage repair/protection, dryness/manageability, or scalp-symptom caution.
