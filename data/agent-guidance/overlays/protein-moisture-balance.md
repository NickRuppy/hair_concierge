# Protein / Moisture Balance

Use when:
- `protein_moisture_balance` is present, the user asks about protein versus moisture, or the answer depends on brittle snapping, mushy/stretchy feel, weakness, stiffness, or mask/conditioner balance.

Advisor interpretation:
- Treat the stored balance as a directional care signal, not a clinical diagnosis.
- In current logic, `snaps` points more toward moisture/conditioning support, `stretches_stays` points more toward protein/strength support, and `stretches_bounces` is a balanced signal.
- Cross-check chemical treatment, heat, breakage, cuticle roughness, and current product use before escalating.
- Keep protein and moisture language practical: feel, manageability, strength support, and avoiding overcorrection.

Category implications:
- Shampoo: Usually not the main protein/moisture lever; avoid choosing shampoo by balance unless product data explicitly supports that role.
- Conditioner: Relevant when balance direction is part of the category target; explain it as length care, not diagnosis.
- Leave-in: Useful only when its supported role or product data fits the balance need; do not infer protein/moisture claims from names alone.
- Mask: Often the clearest balance lever; choose protein, moisture, or balanced support according to the stored signal and damage context.
- Oil: Not a protein or moisture treatment; frame it as lubrication/finish only when otherwise appropriate.
- Bondbuilder / repair: Separate from protein/moisture care; consider when structural damage signals justify a repair lane.

Routine implications:
- Adjust one balance-relevant step at a time, then observe feel after a few uses.
- If hair feels stiff, rough, coated, or worse after a balance change, consider dose, frequency, buildup, or wrong balance before adding more steps.

Avoid:
- Do not overrule the stored balance based only on frizz or generic dryness.
- Do not present stretch/pull-test language as definitive science.
- Do not create protein panic or imply protein is always bad for dry hair.
- Do not stack multiple protein or repair steps without a clear damage reason.

Ask only if:
- current product balance, recent changes, or wet/dry feel would materially change whether to suggest protein, moisture, balanced care, or a reset.

Proactive next step:
- Offer to translate the balance signal into a conditioner or mask lane without changing product claims beyond the supported data.
