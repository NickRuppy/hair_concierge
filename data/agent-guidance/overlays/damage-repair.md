# Damage Repair

Use when:
- bleach, oxidative color, relaxer, perm, keratin treatment, repeated hot tools, breakage, mushy wet feel, high elasticity, split ends, or rough fragile lengths are relevant

Advisor interpretation:
- Separate structural damage from dryness and buildup.
- Bond builders, protein, conditioning, protection, trimming, and behavior changes have different jobs; do not flatten them into "repair."
- Repair products can improve strength, feel, and resilience, but cannot restore hair to virgin condition.
- Split ends need trimming; surrounding hair can still be protected and strengthened.

Category implications:
- Shampoo: Keep cleansing compatible with fragile lengths; do not imply shampoo alone repairs structural damage unless supported by product data.
- Conditioner: Use as friction and manageability support for damaged lengths; it is basic care, not structural reversal.
- Leave-in: Useful for protection, slip, and heat-protection gaps when styling stress is ongoing.
- Mask: Supplemental cosmetic length care after shampoo and before conditioner; keep it off the scalp unless a scalp-specific product is explicitly supported.
- Oil: Optional finish for feel and friction on ends; it does not repair bonds or reverse split ends.
- Bondbuilder / repair: Consider when chemical, heat, or breakage signals justify it; explain the repair lane without promising permanent restoration.

Routine implications:
- Reduce ongoing stress first: heat, rough detangling, tight styles, and repeated chemical processing can outrun any product benefit.
- Use masks, bond builders, or protein-like care as optional targeted additions, not mandatory baseline steps.

Avoid:
- Do not promise permanent repair, regrowth, or split-end reversal.
- Do not recommend bond builders for every dry or frizzy complaint.
- Do not ignore heat and mechanical behavior when damage is ongoing.
- Do not present masks as conditioner replacements or future-damage prevention.

Ask only if:
- the answer depends on whether damage is chemical, heat-driven, mechanical, or mostly dryness.

Proactive next step:
- Offer to separate the next step into repair, moisture/conditioning, heat protection, and trimming priorities.
