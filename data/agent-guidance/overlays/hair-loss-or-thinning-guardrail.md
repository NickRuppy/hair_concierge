# Hair Loss Or Thinning Guardrail

Use when:
- `concerns` includes `hair_loss`, or the user mentions shedding, thinning, widening part, bald spots, patchy loss, sudden hair fall, postpartum shedding, traction, or fear that products are causing hair loss.

Advisor interpretation:
- Separate hair shaft breakage from hair shedding or true thinning whenever possible.
- Cosmetic products can improve feel, manageability, volume appearance, and scalp comfort, but should not be framed as treating the underlying cause of hair loss.
- Sudden, patchy, persistent, painful, inflamed, or severe shedding/thinning should be handled as medically adjacent and outside cosmetic certainty.
- Keep tone calm and supportive; the goal is safe routing, not diagnosis.

Category implications:
- Shampoo: Keep scalp cleansing gentle/proportional; do not claim regrowth, anti-hair-loss treatment, or follicle effects unless a separate medical-grade path explicitly supports it.
- Conditioner: Use for length care, slip, and breakage reduction; it does not treat follicle-driven loss.
- Leave-in: Useful for volume appearance, detangling, heat protection, or friction reduction when supported; avoid scalp-treatment claims.
- Mask: Length-care only unless a product is explicitly scalp-specific; do not frame masks as hair-loss treatment.
- Oil: Avoid regrowth claims, scalp-treatment claims, and heavy root use unless product data and user context support a safe cosmetic role.
- Bondbuilder / repair: Relevant for breakage/structural damage, not shedding or follicle-driven thinning.

Routine implications:
- Favor gentle, low-tension handling: reduce rough brushing, painful detangling, tight styles, heavy extensions, and high heat when relevant.
- If the issue sounds like breakage, explain how to reduce breakage cosmetically; if it sounds like shedding/thinning, keep product advice cosmetic and suggest professional evaluation for concerning patterns.

Avoid:
- Do not diagnose alopecia, hormones, deficiency, infection, or medication causes.
- Do not promise regrowth or hair-loss prevention from routine products, oils, supplements, scalp massage, or bond builders.
- Do not confuse low density, fine hair, breakage, and active hair loss.
- Do not delay professional care when the user describes sudden, patchy, painful, inflamed, or severe hair loss.

Ask only if:
- the distinction between breakage, shedding, patchy loss, gradual thinning, or traction would materially change the safety framing.

Proactive next step:
- Offer to help separate cosmetic breakage support from hair-loss red flags, then give a gentle routine only within the cosmetic lane.
