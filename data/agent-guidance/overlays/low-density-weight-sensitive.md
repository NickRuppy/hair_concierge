# Low Density / Weight Sensitive

Use when:
- `density` is `low`, or the user says they have little hair, sparse-looking hair, visible scalp from low density, easy flatness, or products weigh the hair down quickly.

Advisor interpretation:
- Density is how much hair is present, not strand diameter; do not conflate it with `thickness` or active hair loss.
- Low density usually lowers total product capacity: the same amount of conditioner, leave-in, mask, or oil can look heavier faster.
- Prioritize placement, amount, and layer count before assuming the user needs less care overall.
- If the user describes new shedding, widening part, bald spots, or sudden change, use the hair-loss guardrail instead of treating it as ordinary low density.

Category implications:
- Shampoo: Keep scalp cleansing clean and proportional; volume or freshness framing can matter more than richer scalp care.
- Conditioner: Prefer light to medium weight, mid-length/end placement, and small amounts; avoid skipping conditioner automatically.
- Leave-in: Use sparingly and choose one clear role such as heat protection, detangling, or light smoothing rather than stacking layers.
- Mask: Optional and usually light or occasional; avoid heavy all-over use unless dryness/damage clearly needs it.
- Oil: High caution; use only a tiny amount on ends or skip when flatness, coating, or greasiness is the complaint.
- Bondbuilder / repair: Not driven by density alone; consider only when damage or chemical/heat stress signals justify it.

Routine implications:
- Keep routines lean, use smaller amounts, avoid root-heavy placement, and prefer one multifunctional leave-on step when possible.
- Explain that low density can still need moisture, repair, heat protection, or curl support; the adjustment is dose and placement.

Avoid:
- Do not diagnose thinning or hair loss from low density alone.
- Do not equate low density with fine hair; both can coexist but are separate profile dimensions.
- Do not recommend heavy layering, root oils, or rich masks by default.
- Do not remove all conditioning support just because the hair gets flat.

Ask only if:
- it is unclear whether the user means lifelong low density, active shedding/thinning, or fine strand diameter, and that distinction would change the answer.

Proactive next step:
- Offer an amount-and-placement version of the recommendation so the user gets support without losing volume.
