# Curly Hair

Use when:
- `hair_texture = curly`
- or the user explicitly mentions curls, curl definition, refresh, clumping, shrinkage, curl-related frizz, or dry brushing issues

Advisor interpretation:
- Treat definition, slip, friction control, and product distribution as first-class goals.
- Prefer technique-first advice before adding richer products.
- For refresh questions, think reactivate and reshape before adding more product.
- If `overlay:fine_hair` also applies, preserve curl support while avoiding heavy, flattening products.

Category implications:
- Shampoo: Keep cleansing scalp-focused while avoiding unnecessary stripping of lengths; consider buildup when curls lose definition or feel coated.
- Conditioner: Use for slip, detangling, clumping support, and friction reduction; placement and rinse-out amount matter.
- Leave-in: Useful when curls need light hold-adjacent care, refresh support, or frizz control; keep weight cautious for fine hair.
- Mask: Optional supplemental length care when curls remain rough, dry, or hard to detangle; not automatically required.
- Oil: Optional tiny finishing amount for ends or frizz; avoid making oil a default curl requirement.
- Bondbuilder / repair: Relevant only when damage, chemical processing, heat, or breakage signals exist beyond ordinary frizz.

Routine implications:
- Lead with water, sectioning, scrunching, gentle drying, and refresh technique before expanding the product stack.
- For fine curls, prioritize distribution and lightweight layering over richer products.

Avoid:
- Do not assume curly hair automatically needs heavy masks or oils.
- Do not recommend brushing dry curls as a default.
- Do not treat frizz as always dryness; it can be technique, damage, humidity, buildup, or insufficient hold.
- Do not drift into hold gels or styling-product layering unless the user asks for styling.

Ask only if:
- the answer depends on whether the user wants definition, less frizz, easier detangling, or refresh between washes.

Proactive next step:
- Offer a curl-specific application or refresh sequence using the products the user already has.
