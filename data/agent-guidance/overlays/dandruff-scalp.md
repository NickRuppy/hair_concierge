# Dandruff Scalp

Use when:
- the user explicitly says dandruff, Anti-Schuppen, greasy or yellowish flakes, recurring flakes with oily scalp, or asks about anti-dandruff shampoo

Advisor interpretation:
- Prevent two bad answers: treating dandruff as dryness/oiling, and treating every flaky scalp as a medical emergency.
- Anti-dandruff shampoo logic belongs on the scalp, while lengths may need protection.
- For dry, fragile, curly, coily, bleached, or damaged lengths, CWC/OWC can be a small optional length-protection side recommendation; it protects lengths around the scalp-focused shampoo and does not replace anti-dandruff shampoo.
- In DACH/EU context, do not recommend zinc pyrithione as a normal cosmetic shampoo active.
- Use together with `overlay:sensitive_scalp` only when there is itch, redness, burning, pain, sores, persistent irritation, or inflamed-looking scalp.

Category implications:
- Shampoo: Keep anti-dandruff framing scalp-focused and conservative; avoid medical treatment claims from catalog products.
- Conditioner: Keep on lengths/ends unless a product is explicitly scalp-suitable; do not use conditioner as the dandruff lever.
- Leave-in: Keep away from flaky or irritated scalp; use only for lengths if dryness, frizz, or damage needs it.
- Mask: Optional length protection around scalp-focused washing; do not present masks as dandruff care.
- Oil: Do not suggest scalp oils as the default dandruff fix.
- Bondbuilder / repair: Separate length damage from scalp flakes; repair products are not dandruff tools.

Routine implications:
- Anchor the routine around scalp cleansing and clear rinse-out, then add optional length protection only when the profile supports it.
- If symptoms are painful, inflamed, persistent, or unusual, stay conservative and suggest professional evaluation without diagnosing.

Avoid:
- Do not load this from dry flakes alone unless the user frames the issue as dandruff.
- Do not present CWC/OWC as required for every dandruff user.
- Do not suggest scalp oils as the default dandruff fix.
- Do not turn catalog recommendations into medical treatment claims.

Ask only if:
- flakes could be dry scalp versus dandruff and the user did not make the concern clear enough.

Proactive next step:
- Offer a scalp-focused wash plan with optional length protection for dry or damaged ends.
