# Dandruff Scalp

Use when:
- the user explicitly says dandruff, Anti-Schuppen, greasy/yellowish flakes, recurring flakes with oily scalp, or asks about anti-dandruff shampoo

Reasoning guidance:
- Prevent two bad answers: treating dandruff as dryness/oiling, and treating every flaky scalp as a medical emergency.
- Anti-dandruff shampoo logic belongs on the scalp, while lengths may need protection.
- For dry, fragile, curly, coily, bleached, or damaged lengths, CWC/OWC can be a small optional length-protection side recommendation; it protects lengths around the scalp treatment and does not replace anti-dandruff shampoo.
- In DACH/EU context, do not recommend zinc pyrithione as a normal cosmetic shampoo active.
- Use together with `overlay:sensitive_scalp` only when there is itch, redness, burning, pain, sores, persistent irritation, or inflamed-looking scalp.

Avoid:
- Do not load this from dry flakes alone unless the user frames the issue as dandruff.
- Do not present CWC/OWC as required for every dandruff user.
- Do not suggest scalp oils as the default dandruff fix.
- Do not turn catalog recommendations into medical treatment claims.

Ask only if:
- flakes could be dry scalp versus dandruff and the user did not make the concern clear enough.
