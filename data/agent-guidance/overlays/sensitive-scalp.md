# Sensitive Scalp

Use when:
- the user mentions itch, redness, irritation, burning, pain, tenderness, sores, persistent inflammation, active flaky symptoms, or scalp discomfort

Advisor interpretation:
- Keep scalp comfort and safety above cosmetic product optimization.
- Prefer gentle, low-irritation advice and fewer aggressive steps.
- Pause oils, scrubs, aggressive exfoliation, harsh scalp tools, and strong fragrance/active stacking when irritation is active.
- Distinguish cosmetic discomfort from medical-adjacent symptoms without diagnosing.

Category implications:
- Shampoo: Prefer gentle, conservative scalp framing; anti-dandruff logic belongs here only when dandruff is explicit.
- Conditioner: Keep on lengths unless scalp suitability is explicitly supported.
- Leave-in: Keep away from irritated scalp; use length-only if dryness, tangles, or heat protection need it.
- Mask: Length care only unless a scalp-specific product is explicitly supported; avoid scalp mask claims.
- Oil: Pause scalp oils during active irritation; do not use oil as the default calming answer.
- Bondbuilder / repair: Keep repair products length-focused; they are not scalp treatment.

Routine implications:
- Reduce variables first: simplify the scalp routine, avoid active stacking, rinse well, and reintroduce optional steps cautiously.
- For painful, inflamed, weeping, pustular, patchy-loss, or persistent symptoms, move away from routine optimization and suggest professional evaluation.

Avoid:
- Do not treat painful, inflamed, weeping, pustular, or patchy-loss scalp as a routine problem.
- Do not recommend aggressive scalp massage, exfoliation, or harsh scalp tools.
- Do not make simple dandruff the whole overlay; use `overlay:dandruff_scalp` when dandruff is explicit.
- Do not make medical claims or diagnose scalp conditions.

Ask only if:
- symptoms could be dry scalp, dandruff, allergy/irritation, or another scalp issue and the distinction changes the next step.

Proactive next step:
- Offer a conservative scalp reset: fewer scalp products, gentle cleansing, length-only conditioning, and symptom-aware escalation language.
