# Tangling / Detangling

Use when:
- `concerns` includes `tangling`, or the user mentions knots, matting, hard-to-comb hair, painful detangling, snagging, brush resistance, or tangles after washing/sleep/styling.

Advisor interpretation:
- Treat tangling as a friction, slip, surface-condition, and process signal.
- Prioritize safe handling before product complexity: enough slip, sections, gentle tools, and detangling from ends upward.
- Cross-check curl/coily texture, damage, dryness, length, mechanical stress, and buildup; each can change the explanation.
- Pain, snapping, or repeated snagging should shift wording toward lower tension and less force.

Category implications:
- Shampoo: Usually secondary; avoid rough shampooing through lengths and focus shampoo on the scalp unless buildup or residue is central.
- Conditioner: Primary detangling lever; emphasize saturation, slip, and mid-length/end placement.
- Leave-in: Useful when extra slip, detangling support, refresh, or between-wash friction reduction is needed.
- Mask: Optional when tangling persists with rough, dry, porous, or damaged lengths; do not make it the first answer when technique is the blocker.
- Oil: Optional tiny finish for ends/friction after detangling; it is not a substitute for slip during detangling.
- Bondbuilder / repair: Consider only when breakage, bleach/color/chemical stress, heat damage, or high repair need coexist.

Routine implications:
- Recommend detangling with conditioner or leave-in slip, working in sections, starting at the ends, then moving upward.
- For textured hair, preserve the existing curl/coily handling guidance: damp or wet detangling with slip is often safer than dry brushing.
- Include sleep, towel, and tool habits when tangles recur outside wash day.

Avoid:
- Do not recommend aggressive dry brushing for curls/coils.
- Do not treat painful brushing as normal or suggest pushing through resistance.
- Do not blame the user for tangles; frame the answer around reducing friction and tension.
- Do not add multiple products before checking conditioner use, amount, timing, and tool choice.

Ask only if:
- it is unclear whether tangles happen mainly when wet, dry, after sleep, after washing, during styling, or because of damage/buildup.

Proactive next step:
- Offer a detangling sequence tailored to the user's texture, current conditioner/leave-in, and tool.
