# Frizz Control

Use when:
- `concerns` includes `frizz`, `goals` includes `less_frizz`, or the user mentions frizz, flyaways, puffiness, halo frizz, rough surface feel, or style expansion.

Advisor interpretation:
- Treat frizz as a symptom pattern, not a diagnosis.
- Frizz can come from dryness, rough cuticle, damage, friction, humidity, buildup, insufficient conditioning, or styling technique.
- Start from the surrounding profile signals: texture, dryness, damage, buildup risk, heat use, and whether the user wants smoothness or curl definition.
- Keep wording practical and observable rather than promising permanent smoothing.

Category implications:
- Shampoo: Not the main frizz lever unless buildup, oiliness, or rough washing is involved; do not present shampoo alone as an anti-frizz fix.
- Conditioner: Use for slip, softness, and surface manageability on lengths; scale richness by fine hair, density, and buildup risk.
- Leave-in: Useful for smoothing, detangling, curl support, heat protection, or humidity-adjacent styling support when product data supports the role.
- Mask: Optional when frizz is paired with dry, rough, damaged, or hard-to-detangle lengths; not mandatory for all frizz.
- Oil: Optional tiny finishing amount for ends or surface feel; keep away from roots and avoid it when flatness or coating is the issue.
- Bondbuilder / repair: Relevant only when frizz coexists with credible structural damage, bleach/color/chemical stress, heat damage, or breakage.

Routine implications:
- Favor low-friction handling, enough conditioner slip, wet or damp product placement when relevant, and drying methods that do not roughen the surface.
- For curly or coily hair, pair frizz advice with definition and clumping technique rather than forcing a smooth-straight frame.
- If buildup is plausible, simplify or reset before adding richer anti-frizz layers.

Avoid:
- Do not equate frizz with dryness every time.
- Do not promise permanent frizz removal or salon-level smoothing from routine changes.
- Do not ignore curl pattern, humidity, buildup, or mechanical stress when they are the likely driver.
- Do not make oil, masks, or bond builders the default answer to frizz.

Ask only if:
- the answer depends on whether the frizz feels dry/rough, coated/heavy, damage-related, curl-technique-related, or mainly caused by heat/humidity.

Proactive next step:
- Offer a short frizz triage or a one-change routine tweak that separates moisture, buildup, technique, and damage.
