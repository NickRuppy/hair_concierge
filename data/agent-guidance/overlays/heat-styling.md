# Heat Styling

Use when:
- `heat_styling` is not `never`
- `styling_tools` includes hot tools
- or the user mentions straightening, curling iron, hot brush, high-heat blow-drying, heat protectant, or heat damage

Advisor interpretation:
- Separate heat protection from repair: protection reduces future stress; it does not undo existing damage.
- If heat is frequent and no heat protectant is present, this is a high-priority behavior or product gap.
- Prefer a leave-in that already includes heat protection when it also fits the user's care target, rather than adding a separate product by default.
- Use the lowest effective temperature framing, especially for fine or damaged hair.

Category implications:
- Shampoo: Not the main heat lever; keep cleansing compatible with scalp and length condition.
- Conditioner: Supports slip and friction reduction for heat-stressed lengths, but does not replace heat protection.
- Leave-in: Primary category for combined care plus heat protection when product data supports it; acknowledge separate heat protectant if already used.
- Mask: Optional supplemental length care for heat-stressed or rough lengths; it does not undo heat damage.
- Oil: Do not treat ordinary oil as heat protection unless product data explicitly supports heat use; small finishing use belongs after styling.
- Bondbuilder / repair: Consider for repeated heat plus breakage, snapping, or fragile lengths; keep it optional and evidence-limited.

Routine implications:
- Prioritize protectant use, lower effective temperature, full drying before irons, less frequent hot-tool use when possible, and gentle handling.
- If the user only diffuses gently, do not escalate to high-risk hot-tool advice unless other signals support it.

Avoid:
- Do not claim heat causes permanent follicle hair loss.
- Do not imply heat protectant makes high heat harmless.
- Do not load this from diffuser use alone unless explicit heat-risk signals are present.
- Do not present repair products as a substitute for changing ongoing heat behavior.

Ask only if:
- the user asks for a specific product and we do not know whether they air-dry, blow-dry, diffuse, or use hot tools.

Proactive next step:
- Offer a heat-styling routine check: protectant, amount, drying state, temperature, and aftercare.
