# Heat Styling

Use when:
- `heat_styling` is not `never`
- `styling_tools` includes hot tools
- or the user mentions straightening, curling iron, hot brush, high-heat blow-drying, or heat damage

Reasoning guidance:
- Separate heat protection from repair: protection reduces future stress; it does not undo existing damage.
- If heat is frequent and no heat protectant is present, this is a high-priority behavior or product gap.
- Prefer a leave-in that already includes heat protection when it also fits the user's care target, rather than adding a separate product by default.
- Use the lowest effective temperature framing, especially for fine or damaged hair.

Avoid:
- Do not claim heat causes permanent follicle hair loss.
- Do not imply heat protectant makes high heat harmless.
- Do not load this from diffuser use alone unless explicit heat-risk signals are present.

Ask only if:
- the user asks for a specific product and we do not know whether they air-dry, blow-dry, or use hot tools.
