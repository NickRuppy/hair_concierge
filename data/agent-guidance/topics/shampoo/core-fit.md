# Shampoo: Core Fit

## Runtime Variables

- `profile.thickness`
- `profile.scalp_type`
- `profile.scalp_condition`
- `profile.concerns`
- `current_routine_products`

## Category Role

Shampoo is primarily a scalp and root cleansing category. It supports freshness, root feel, buildup control, and scalp comfort, while lengths are usually handled by rinse-down rather than direct shampoo work.

## Best Fit

- oily roots, freshness needs, visible residue, or buildup signals
- scalp/root cleansing questions, including wash feel and cleanser strength
- flakes, irritation, or scalp-condition mentions when framed conservatively and without treating medical scalp disease as a product-choice problem
- explicit shampoo requests where the best-fit shampoo can be discussed with caveats

## Weak Fit

- dry lengths, frizz, shine, and split-end repair when shampoo is the only proposed lever
- length softness or damage-repair goals that are better served by conditioner, leave-in, mask, or styling
- medical scalp concerns that need cautious routing rather than product promises

## Decision Axes

- `cleansing_lane`: scalp/root freshness, buildup control, oil management, or comfort
- `strength_tolerance`: thickness, scalp type, scalp condition, and routine residue decide how gentle or resetting the shampoo lane should be
- `length_mismatch`: dry lengths, frizz, shine, or split ends should trigger soft steering toward length-care categories
- `routine_context`: current products decide whether shampoo is the missing step, a reset support, or already covered

## Profile Interplay

Fine or easily weighed-down hair may need a cleaner root feel without making lengths the shampoo target. Dry, sensitive, curly, or chemically stressed profiles need extra caution around stronger cleansing and should usually keep length-care advice in conditioner, leave-in, or mask lanes.

## Compare Against Other Categories

- Compare against conditioner when the user wants everyday softness, slip, or less friction after washing.
- Compare against leave-in when the user wants post-wash frizz control, styling prep, or supported heat-protection consolidation.
- Compare against mask when the user wants occasional extra conditioning for rough lengths.
- Compare against deep cleansing when the issue is buildup, residue, or a reset rather than normal cleansing.

## Answer Guidance

- Tell the user to apply shampoo mainly to the scalp and roots.
- Let rinse-down handle lengths unless there is heavy product residue.
- When the user explicitly asks for shampoo for dry lengths, frizz, shine, or split ends, answer the shampoo question but softly explain that another category is likely the stronger lever.
- Do not name products unless the active tool path has selected products.

## Guardrails

- Do not frame shampoo as a length-repair product.
- Do not promise split-end repair, structural repair, or medical scalp treatment.
- Keep scalp-disease-adjacent language conservative and route severe, persistent, painful, or inflamed symptoms away from cosmetic product certainty.
