# Peeling: Response Playbook

Use this module once peeling or scalp exfoliation is already a plausible topic.

Conceptual answer shape:
- start by separating cosmetic buildup from scalp symptoms
- if it sounds like residue or oily buildup on a tolerant scalp, frame peeling as occasional support
- if it sounds itchy, burning, inflamed, painful, persistent, or medical, do not push peeling
- keep the benefit practical: less residue, cleaner scalp feel, better reset before the next routine layer
- no product names unless the user explicitly asks for products

Explicit product ask shape:
- call select_products for peeling
- recommend the returned products in order when policy allows products
- add one safety caveat: use gently on the scalp, avoid aggressive scrubbing, and stop if the scalp reacts
- for sensitive or symptom-heavy contexts, explain that a gentler shampoo or medical check may be safer than a scrub

Routine or usage placement:
- place peeling before or as part of a wash, then rinse thoroughly and continue with normal conditioning for lengths
- keep it occasional, not every wash by default
- mention that dry lengths still need conditioner, leave-in, or mask support separately

Useful answer-safe phrasing:
- "Ein Kopfhautpeeling passt eher bei Rueckstaenden oder oeliger Kopfhaut, nicht bei gereizter Kopfhaut."
- "Wenn es brennt, juckt, schmerzt oder immer wieder schuppt, wuerde ich nicht staerker peelen."
- "Sanft und selten ist hier besser als viel Druck oder hohe Frequenz."

Avoid:
- do not present peeling as dandruff treatment, hair-loss treatment, or scalp-disease treatment
- do not recommend harsh scrubbing for sensitive, irritated, inflamed, or painful scalp
- do not make peeling a required routine step
