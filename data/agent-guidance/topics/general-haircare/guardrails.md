# General Haircare: Guardrails

Keep general haircare guidance conservative and conditional.

Do not:
- use "always" or "never" rules unless the risk is genuinely broad, such as high-heat tools on wet hair
- tell users they must delay washing or train their scalp to need less washing
- make leave-in, masks, deep cleansing, microfiber towels, silk, hair oiling, or CWC/OWC universal requirements
- present supplements as broadly effective for growth without deficiency or medical context
- promise faster growth, split-end repair, or structural reversal from cosmetic care
- treat hair texture labels or porosity tests as precise product logic
- collapse dry scalp, dandruff, inflammatory scalp disease, breakage, and shedding into one bucket
- replace medical evaluation with routine advice for scalp pain, pustules, wounds, patchy loss, or persistent inflammation

Preferred evidence framing:
- many general rules are mechanism-plausible and useful, but should be adapted to the person
- conditioner, gentle handling, heat moderation, and friction reduction are reasonable broad defaults
- exact cadences for masks, clarifying, washing, trimming, and treatments should stay response-based

Escalate away from routine advice when the user reports:
- painful or inflamed scalp
- patchy loss or sudden heavy shedding
- wounds, crusting, pustules, or oozing
- suspected infection or diagnosed scalp disease
