# General Haircare: Response Playbook

Use this module after general-haircare relevance is plausible.

Default answer shape:
- start with the user's actual goal, not a full routine lecture
- give 2-4 practical principles, ordered by impact
- phrase advice as conditional where hair type, scalp state, or damage level matters
- link the advice to what the user can observe: buildup, oiliness, tangles, breakage, roughness, irritation, or weight
- avoid turning optional routines into universal requirements

Good reasons to ask one follow-up:
- the answer changes based on scalp state versus length damage
- the user might mean shedding rather than breakage
- texture changes the advice, especially wet versus dry detangling
- product use, heat use, or water hardness is central to the issue

Useful answer-safe phrasing:
- "For most people, shampoo belongs mainly on the scalp; the lengths often get enough from the rinse-down."
- "Wash frequency should match your scalp and lifestyle. There is no need to train your hair to tolerate less washing."
- "Conditioner helps many routines, but placement matters, especially for fine or oily hair."
- "This sounds like a care habit question, not a product-stack problem yet."

If a narrower topic is clearly more relevant, load or defer to that topic instead of answering only from this general baseline.
