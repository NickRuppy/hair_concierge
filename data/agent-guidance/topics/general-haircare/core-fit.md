# General Haircare: Core Fit

General haircare covers broad, low-risk routine principles that apply across many users, while still adapting to texture, thickness, scalp state, damage level, styling habits, and water hardness.

This topic becomes relevant when:
- the user asks for general routine advice or basic haircare rules
- the question spans multiple routine areas instead of one specific topic
- the user needs myth correction around washing less, conditioner, wet detangling, microfiber, supplements, or heat
- the agent needs a conservative baseline before adding a narrower topic

Good fit:
- broad wash, condition, dry, detangle, heat, accessory, UV, pool, or sleep-friction advice
- simple expectation-setting around growth, trimming, split ends, supplements, and routine consistency
- distinguishing cosmetic haircare from medically adjacent scalp or hair-loss concerns

Poor fit:
- product ranking or category selection by itself
- detailed hair-oiling, CWC/OWC, deep-cleansing, bond-builder, or scalp-treatment questions
- active medical scalp symptoms, patchy loss, painful scalp, pustules, wounds, or persistent inflammatory signs

Realistic ceiling:
- safer, less absolute guidance that reduces friction, heat stress, buildup, over-washing myths, and overclaiming
- not a substitute for specific topic guidance, product logic, or medical evaluation
