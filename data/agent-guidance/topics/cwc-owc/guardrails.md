# CWC & OWC: Guardrails

Keep CWC and OWC guidance conservative.

Do not:
- present CWC or OWC as clinically proven protocols
- treat them as structural repair, split-end repair, or regrowth techniques
- recommend OWC for diagnosed or strongly suspected scalp disease without redirecting first
- collapse these techniques into co-washing or standalone hair oiling
- impose one fixed cadence such as every wash or every second wash
- claim a quantified color-longevity benefit from the technique alone
- ignore shampoo choice when color preservation is the user's actual goal
- recommend heavy OWC by default for fine, easily weighed-down hair

Preferred evidence framing:
- mechanism-plausible
- practitioner-validated
- limited direct protocol-level evidence

Escalate away from technique advice when the user reports:
- persistent flaking, itching, burning, or scalp inflammation
- scalp pain or suspected medical scalp disease
- repeated greasy or coated results even after light technique adjustments

In those cases, the next step is to address scalp care, shampoo choice, or medical evaluation before intensifying CWC or OWC.
