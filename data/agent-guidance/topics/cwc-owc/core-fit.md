# CWC & OWC: Core Fit

CWC and OWC are wash techniques, not product categories. Both protect lengths by putting a film on the mid-lengths and ends before shampoo touches them, then focusing shampoo on the scalp instead of scrubbing the full length.

Default fit:
- CWC usually fits straight or wavy hair with fine to normal thickness, especially when the scalp needs frequent washing but the lengths dry out
- OWC usually fits curly or coily hair, coarse hair, or very dry and damaged lengths that need more slip and wash protection
- Both are useful when the user has dry or color-treated lengths, frequent wash days, or a scalp-and-length mismatch such as oily roots with dry ends

Poor fit or not needed:
- medical scalp conditions, persistent flaking, itching, or inflammatory scalp symptoms
- very short hair where scalp and lengths cannot really be separated
- users whose main problem is structural damage, regrowth, or scalp disease
- users who already feel heavy, coated, or not-clean-enough after washing and are unlikely to tolerate more pre-wash product

What they can realistically help with:
- less stripped-feeling lengths on frequent wash days
- less friction and roughness during detangling
- gentler washing for dry, porous, or color-treated lengths
- slower color fade in a mechanism-plausible sense, especially when paired with a gentler shampoo

What they do not solve:
- structural repair
- medical scalp disease
- regrowth or hair-loss concerns
- already-faded color
- a clearly wrong shampoo choice

Realistic ceiling: these techniques can make wash days feel gentler and lengths look better over repeated cycles, but they are protection-during-washing techniques, not repair protocols.
