# CWC & OWC: Confusions

Users often mix CWC and OWC up with adjacent wash or oiling techniques. Keep the boundaries clear.

What this topic is:
- CWC means conditioner on the lengths first, then scalp-focused shampoo, then regular post-wash conditioner
- OWC means oil on the lengths first, then scalp-focused shampoo, then regular post-wash conditioner

Important boundary:
- both techniques still include a real shampoo step at the scalp

Common confusions:
- co-washing
- cleansing conditioners
- standalone pre-wash hair oiling
- generic "reverse washing" language

How to explain the difference:
- co-washing replaces shampoo with a cleansing conditioner, while CWC and OWC still shampoo the scalp
- cleansing conditioners are products, not the technique itself
- standalone pre-wash oiling is a longer separate oiling step with its own cadence, while OWC compresses oil protection into the wash session itself
- "reverse washing" is usually just another name for the conditioner-first logic of CWC

If the user mainly wants detailed oil choice, dwell time, or standalone oiling advice, that belongs in the hair-oiling topic rather than here.
