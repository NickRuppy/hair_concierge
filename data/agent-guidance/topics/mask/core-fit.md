# Mask: Core Fit

## Runtime Variables

- `profile.thickness`
- `profile.hair_texture`
- `profile.chemical_treatment`
- `profile.protein_moisture_balance`
- `profile.concerns`

## Category Role

Mask is periodic extra care for lengths and ends. It can support more intensive conditioning for rough-feeling lengths, but it is not a required baseline step for every user.

## Best Fit

- dry, rough, frizzy, chemically treated, or more stressed lengths that need occasional extra conditioning
- users whose baseline conditioner is present but not enough for length feel
- need-based routines where an occasional treatment step is acceptable
- protein/moisture balance questions where the mask role can be kept conservative

## Weak Fit

- scalp symptoms or root cleansing needs
- replacing conditioner by default
- permanent repair of split ends or structural damage
- minimalist or fine-hair routines where extra weight is likely not needed

## Decision Axes

- `care_intensity`: thickness, texture, treatment history, and concerns decide how much extra conditioning is useful
- `protein_moisture_direction`: protein_moisture_balance influences cautious wording around protein-rich versus moisture-leaning masks
- `cadence`: occasional or need-based use, not a universal daily step
- `placement`: lengths and ends only; avoid scalp unless product directions explicitly say otherwise

## Profile Interplay

Fine hair and simple routines may need the mask kept optional or used less often. Curly, coarse, chemically treated, dry, or rough-feeling lengths may justify more intensive periodic care. Scalp concerns should route away from mask as the main lever.

## Compare Against Other Categories

- Compare against conditioner when the user lacks a baseline after-wash length-care step.
- Compare against leave-in when the user needs post-wash support, styling prep, or leave-on frizz control.
- Compare against oil when the user asks about tips finish, shine, or pre-wash oil rituals.
- Compare against shampoo or deep cleansing when the issue is cleansing, buildup, or root feel.

## Answer Guidance

- Position mask as optional extra care, not the default routine foundation.
- Use after shampoo and before conditioner unless product directions say otherwise.
- Apply to lengths and ends and keep cadence occasional or need-based.
- Be explicit that rough-feeling lengths may feel softer or better conditioned, but split ends are not permanently repaired.

## Guardrails

- Do not promise structural repair or permanent split-end repair.
- Do not treat mask as a scalp treatment.
- Do not make mask mandatory for every routine.
