# Dry Shampoo: Response Playbook

Use this module once dry shampoo is already a plausible topic.

Conceptual answer shape:
- start with the bridge role: it can make oily roots look fresher for a short time
- say plainly that it does not clean the scalp like shampoo and water
- tie fit to the user's signal: oily roots, stretched wash day, post-workout freshness, or time pressure
- keep length-care goals out of this category unless the user also needs root freshness
- no product names unless the user explicitly asks for products

Explicit product ask shape:
- call select_products for dry_shampoo
- recommend the returned products in order when policy allows products
- add one practical caveat: use it at roots, distribute and brush out as directed, and wash it out later
- if the user has scalp irritation, persistent flakes, or soreness, avoid escalating dry shampoo and suggest a gentler scalp-cleansing or professional-care path

Routine or usage placement:
- place it between wet wash days, not as the cleanse step itself
- keep it optional and situational
- avoid making it a daily default for buildup-prone or sensitive scalps

Useful answer-safe phrasing:
- "Trockenshampoo ist eher ein Frische-Bridge als eine echte Waesche."
- "Es kann Oel optisch abpuffern, aber es entfernt Schweiss, Sebum und Rueckstaende nicht wie Shampoo mit Wasser."
- "Wenn du es ein- bis zweimal genutzt hast, plane wieder eine normale Waesche ein."

Avoid:
- do not imply dry shampoo replaces regular shampoo
- do not treat scalp symptoms, dandruff, or hair loss with dry shampoo
- do not promise volume, colour invisibility, or irritation-free use without product facts
