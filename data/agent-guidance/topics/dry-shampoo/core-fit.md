# Dry Shampoo: Core Fit

## Runtime Variables

- `profile.scalp_type`
- `profile.scalp_condition`
- `profile.thickness`
- `profile.concerns`
- `wash_frequency`
- oily-root, freshness, and time-between-washes signals

## Category Role

Dry shampoo is a temporary freshness and oil-absorption bridge between wet washes. It can make roots look and feel less oily for a short period, but it does not clean the scalp the way regular shampoo and water do.

## Best Fit

- oily roots or second-day freshness needs when the user wants to stretch time between wet washes
- fine or flat hair that needs root-side freshness without adding water
- users who need a quick bridge before the next wash day
- post-workout or schedule-constrained situations when there are no irritation or medical scalp signals

## Weak Fit

- replacing regular washing indefinitely
- itchy, burning, inflamed, painful, wounded, or persistently flaky scalp contexts
- heavy buildup, residue, or scalp congestion that needs actual cleansing
- dry lengths, frizz, repair, split ends, or moisture goals

## Decision Axes

- `freshness_bridge`: quick visual oil control versus actual scalp cleansing
- `wash_gap`: occasional bridge is stronger than repeated layering over many days
- `scalp_tolerance`: irritation, pain, persistent flakes, or inflammation should route away from cosmetic escalation
- `weight_visibility_risk`: fine, dark, low-density, or easily weighed-down hair needs careful dose and brush-out framing

## Profile Interplay

Oily roots and fine or flat hair strengthen the dry-shampoo case when the scalp is otherwise comfortable. Sensitive, itchy, flaky, inflamed, or buildup-prone scalps need caution because dry shampoo can add residue and does not remove sweat, sebum, or product film.

## Compare Against Other Categories

- Compare against shampoo when the user needs actual scalp cleansing.
- Compare against deep cleansing when residue or buildup is already the problem.
- Compare against leave-in or oil when the goal is length softness, shine, or frizz control.
- Compare against scalp-care guardrails when symptoms sound persistent, painful, inflamed, or medical.

## Answer Guidance

- Frame dry shampoo as a short-term bridge, not a wash replacement.
- Explain that it absorbs visible oil but does not truly clean the scalp.
- Mention applying mainly at roots, letting it sit if the product directions say so, then massaging and brushing or combing out.
- Keep cadence conservative: avoid indefinite layering and wash with regular shampoo and water after one or two dry-shampoo uses.
- Do not name products unless the active tool path has selected products.

## Guardrails

- Do not say dry shampoo cleans the scalp.
- Do not present it as a fix for dandruff, scalp irritation, hair loss, or buildup.
- Do not encourage repeated layering when the user reports itch, flakes, burning, soreness, or heavy residue.

## Source Note

AAD consumer guidance frames dry shampoo as oil absorption and freshness support, not a substitute for washing with regular shampoo and water, and recommends washing after one or two dry-shampoo uses.
