# Bond Builder: Response Playbook

Use this module after bond-builder relevance is already plausible.

Default answer shape:
- Start with whether bond repair sounds relevant at all
- Explain the expected benefit in plain language: strength and resilience, not instant softness or regrowth
- Keep claims conservative
- Ask at most one follow-up if the answer would change whether or how bond repair should be recommended

Good reasons to ask one follow-up:
- It is unclear whether the damage is structural at all
- It is unclear whether the user recently had bleach, color, relaxing, or heavy heat exposure
- It is unclear whether an in-salon chemical service is coming up soon

Routine placement:
- Present bond building as a targeted repair step, not a daily staple
- Keep the guidance generic: product-specific timing and cadence should follow the product's own directions
- Position it before the user's normal conditioning or masking step when the product format calls for that
- Pair it with moisture support when the hair is also dry

Useful answer-safe phrasing:
- "This sounds more like structural damage than a pure moisture issue, so bond repair may be worth considering."
- "Bond builders can help damaged hair feel stronger and break less easily, but they do not replace conditioner and they do not undo all damage."
- "If a chemical service is coming up, bond repair during the service is usually higher leverage than trying to rescue the hair afterwards."

Avoid loading profile nuance into this file unless it changes first-pass relevance. Curly/coily handling, porosity nuance, and product-format nuance are better as separate overlays or product-specific usage metadata later.
