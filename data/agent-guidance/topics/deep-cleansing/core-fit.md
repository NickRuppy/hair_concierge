# Deep Cleansing: Core Fit

## Runtime Variables

- `profile.scalp_type`
- `profile.concerns`
- `products_used`
- `current_routine_products`
- buildup/reset signals

## Category Role

Deep cleansing is an occasional reset step, not everyday cleansing and not scalp-disease treatment. In practice it covers clarifying for product and silicone buildup, chelating for hard-water, pool, and metal deposits, and scalp exfoliation for oily or mildly flaky scalp buildup.

## Best Fit

- heavy product routines with coated, waxy, limp, oily-feeling, or unresponsive hair
- hard-water or swim exposure, especially with dull, brassy, stiff, or mineral-loaded hair
- users preparing for a chemical service where metal removal matters
- mild non-medical scalp buildup, oiliness, visible residue, or reset language
- film-heavy routines like CWC, OWC, co-washing, or regular oiling that need a reset

## Weak Fit

- daily cleansing or routine-baseline shampoo replacement
- minimalist routines with no buildup or reset signals
- very dry, fragile, fine, curly, color-treated, or sensitive profiles when the only proposed move is a harsh full-length clarifier
- diagnosed or strongly suspected medical scalp disease
- structural repair, regrowth, or already-happened color fade

## Decision Axes

- `reset_signal`: buildup, residue, heavy/oily feel, limp or unresponsive hair, or explicit routine reset
- `variant_fit`: clarifying, chelating, or scalp exfoliation depending on product film, mineral exposure, or mild scalp buildup
- `caution_profile`: dry, fine, curly, color-treated, fragile, or sensitive profiles need gentler and less frequent framing
- `routine_context`: products_used and current_routine_products decide whether buildup is plausible

## Profile Interplay

Oily roots, heavy product use, co-washing, regular oiling, hard-water exposure, or swim exposure can strengthen the reset case. Dry, fine, curly, color-treated, fragile, or sensitive profiles should be handled cautiously and not pushed into frequent or harsh cleansing.

## Compare Against Other Categories

- Compare against shampoo when the user needs normal scalp/root cleansing rather than an occasional reset.
- Compare against conditioner, leave-in, or mask when the problem is dry or rough lengths without buildup signals.
- Compare against oiling when regular oil use may be creating heaviness or residue.
- Compare against scalp medical guidance when symptoms sound inflamed, painful, persistent, or disease-like.

## Answer Guidance

- Frame deep cleansing as occasional and need-based.
- Ask or infer which reset job is relevant: product film, minerals/pool exposure, or mild scalp buildup.
- Explain that hair can feel cleaner, lighter, and more responsive after a proper reset, but buildup returns over time and the right variant matters.
- Do not name products unless the active tool path has selected products.

## Guardrails

- Do not present deep cleansing as daily cleansing.
- Do not treat medical scalp disease, hair loss, structural shaft damage, or color fade as solved by deep cleansing.
- Be cautious with dry, fine, curly, color-treated, fragile, or sensitive profiles.
