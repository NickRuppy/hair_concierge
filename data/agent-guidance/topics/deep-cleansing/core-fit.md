# Deep Cleansing: Core Fit

Deep cleansing is a periodic reset step, not an everyday wash category. In practice it covers three different jobs:
- clarifying for product and silicone buildup
- chelating for hard-water, pool, and metal deposits
- scalp exfoliation for oily or mildly flaky scalp buildup

Good fit:
- heavy product routines with coated, waxy, limp, or unresponsive hair
- hard-water or swim exposure, especially with dull, brassy, stiff, or mineral-loaded hair
- users preparing for a chemical service where metal removal matters
- users with mild non-medical scalp buildup, oiliness, or visible residue
- users on film-heavy routines like CWC, OWC, co-washing, or regular oiling who need a reset

Poor fit or not needed:
- minimalist routines with no buildup signals
- very dry or fragile hair when the only proposed move is a harsh clarifier on the full length
- diagnosed or strongly suspected medical scalp disease
- active scalp wounds, recent procedures, or post-op scalp healing
- users looking for structural repair, regrowth, or a daily routine step

What it can realistically help with:
- remove product film and restore lighter-feeling hair
- remove hard-water or pool-related metal deposits when the product is actually a chelator
- improve scalp cleanliness and reduce mild buildup-driven flaking or oiliness
- make wash-day performance and styling feel more normal again

What it does not solve:
- structural shaft repair
- medical scalp disease
- hair-loss concerns
- already-happened color fade
- a fundamentally wrong routine if the wrong deep-cleansing variant is chosen

Realistic ceiling: hair can feel cleaner, lighter, and more responsive after a proper reset, but buildup returns over time and the right variant matters more than the umbrella label.
