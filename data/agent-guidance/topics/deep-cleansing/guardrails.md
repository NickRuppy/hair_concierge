# Deep Cleansing: Guardrails

Keep deep-cleansing guidance conservative and specific.

Do not:
- collapse clarifying, chelating, and scalp exfoliation into one interchangeable recommendation
- recommend deep cleansing as a weekly-or-more-frequent staple by default
- present deep cleansing as structural repair
- recommend aggressive scalp scrubs or DIY salt and sugar mixes as a general default
- suggest cosmetic deep cleansing as first-line treatment for medical scalp disease
- claim every "clarifying" or "detox" product also removes hard-water metals
- promise quantified color benefits from clarifying alone
- claim deep cleansing "opens the cuticle" as if that were the mechanism

Preferred evidence framing:
- mechanism-plausible
- practitioner-validated
- direct evidence varies by sub-type, with chelating and salicylic-acid scalp care stronger than umbrella "detox" claims

Escalate away from routine advice when the user reports:
- persistent inflammatory scalp symptoms
- diagnosed scalp disease
- repeated over-stripping, straw-like hair, or breakage after reset attempts

In those cases, the next step is to address scalp treatment, deep-cleansing variant choice, or professional evaluation before intensifying the reset step.
