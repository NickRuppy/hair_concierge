# Deep Cleansing: Confusions

This topic is often flattened into one generic "detox" idea. Keep the buckets separate.

What this topic can include:
- clarifying for organic product buildup
- chelating for hard-water, pool, or service-related metal deposits
- scalp exfoliation for mild scalp residue and non-medical buildup

Common confusions:
- calling any clarifying shampoo a chelator
- using chelation language for plain buildup reset
- treating scalp exfoliation as if it cleans the fibre itself
- treating hair oiling as a deep-cleansing equivalent
- assuming every "detox" label means the product does all three jobs

How to explain the difference:
- clarifying removes product film and residue
- chelating binds and removes metals such as calcium, iron, or copper
- scalp exfoliation works on scalp skin and sebum buildup, not on fibre mineral deposits
- hair oiling can soften or loosen sebum in a gentle pre-wash sense, but it is not a clarifier or a chelator

If the user is really asking about standalone oiling, bond repair, or CWC/OWC film buildup, route that part to the relevant topic instead of stretching deep cleansing too far.
