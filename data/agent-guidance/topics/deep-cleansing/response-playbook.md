# Deep Cleansing: Response Playbook

Use this module once deep cleansing is already a plausible topic.

Default answer shape:
- explain first that deep cleansing is a periodic reset, not a standard every-wash step
- identify the likely bucket before naming any action: clarifying, chelating, or scalp exfoliation
- tie the recommendation to the user's actual signal such as waxy buildup, hard-water dullness, pool exposure, or root-side scalp congestion
- keep the upside practical: lighter feel, cleaner scalp, better styling response, or better prep before a service
- ask at most one follow-up if it would change which deep-cleansing variant is appropriate

Good reasons to ask one follow-up:
- it is unclear whether the problem is product buildup, hard-water minerals, or scalp residue
- it is unclear whether the scalp symptoms may be medical rather than cosmetic
- it is unclear whether the user has very dry, porous, or damaged hair that will not tolerate a harsh clarifier well

Routine or usage placement:
- frame deep cleansing as replacing the regular shampoo on a reset wash day
- say clearly that post-wash conditioning is still needed
- keep cadence flexible and response-based rather than universal
- mention pre-service chelating when an oxidative color or bleach service is coming up

Useful answer-safe phrasing:
- "The first question is whether you need a buildup reset, a hard-water reset, or a scalp reset."
- "A clarifier removes product film, while a chelator is the better tool for metals and hard water."
- "This should be occasional maintenance, not your default wash method."

Keep detailed ingredient-label logic, brand examples, and long special-case nuance out of the default runtime topic set.
