# Hair Oiling: Response Playbook

Use this module after hair-oiling relevance is already plausible.

Default answer shape:
- start by naming the goal clearly: pre-wash length protection, scalp comfort, or cautious adjunct use around thinning
- explain the expected upside in plain language: less roughness during washing, calmer lengths, or temporary scalp comfort
- keep claims conservative and separate comfort claims from growth claims
- ask at most one follow-up if the answer would change whether oiling is appropriate or which oil style fits best

Good reasons to ask one follow-up:
- it is unclear whether the scalp issue sounds medical rather than cosmetic
- it is unclear whether the user wants scalp comfort, length protection, or growth support
- it is unclear whether the user's hair is very fine and easily weighed down

Routine or usage placement:
- present hair oiling as a pre-wash step, not a rescue treatment and not a leave-in mask
- explain the core flow simply: apply, massage gently, leave on briefly, shampoo it out properly, then condition or use leave-in as usual
- emphasize wash-out technique because rinsing with water first is the most common failure mode
- frame cadence as wash-count-based and response-based, not as a hard universal schedule

Useful answer-safe phrasing:
- "This is most useful as a pre-wash protection step, not as a repair treatment."
- "If your scalp is dry but not medically inflamed, oiling can improve comfort, but the effect is gradual."
- "If your goal is growth, oiling is at best an adjunct. It is not a substitute for medical evaluation or proven treatments."

Keep deeper oil-selection logic, essential-oil evidence, and scalp-disease nuance out of the default runtime topic set unless a later dedicated module is added.
