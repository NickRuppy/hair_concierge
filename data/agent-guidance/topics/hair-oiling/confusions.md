# Hair Oiling: Common Confusions

Many users and products blur pre-wash hair oiling with other oily-feeling products. This file exists to keep those buckets separate.

High-confidence examples of the real topic:
- pre-wash plant oils dominated by coconut, jojoba, almond, argan, castor, or similar triglyceride or wax-ester oils
- carrier oils used for a short pre-wash scalp or length ritual

Important nuance:
- these are topic examples, not automatic product recommendations
- a product can still be useful cosmetically even if it is not a true pre-wash oiling product

Common look-alikes or confusions:
- silicone-based finishing serums marketed as "hair oil"
- post-shampoo hair masks
- medicated dandruff or scalp-treatment shampoos

How to explain the difference:
- pre-wash oils are meant to sit on the scalp or lengths before shampooing and then be washed out
- finishing serums are leave-in smoothing products for shine and frizz control, not pre-wash protection
- masks are conditioning treatments with a different place in the routine
- medicated dandruff care addresses scalp disease; it is not interchangeable with cosmetic oiling
