# Hair Oiling: Guardrails

Keep hair-oiling guidance conservative.

Do not:
- recommend hair oiling for diagnosed or strongly suspected medical scalp disease without redirecting first
- recommend neat essential-oil application
- promise regrowth, thickness gain, or faster length gain from oiling
- claim oils rebuild structural damage inside the shaft
- claim oils "moisturise" hair as if the oil itself were the water source
- treat coconut oil as universally best for every hair type
- encode one exact essential-oil dilution or one exact cadence as the app-level rule
- collapse hair oiling, silicone finishing serums, and hair masks into the same recommendation

Preferred evidence framing:
- pre-wash protection is mechanism-plausible and partially evidence-backed
- scalp-comfort and massage benefits are practitioner-validated but not strongly proven
- growth-oriented claims from rosemary or peppermint remain limited and should stay conservative

Escalate away from topic advice when the user reports:
- persistent flaking, burning, stinging, or pustules
- painful scalp, patchy loss, or clearly inflammatory symptoms
- prior essential-oil sensitisation or repeated irritation from scalp products

In those cases, the next step is:
- stop giving routine-style oiling advice and redirect to medical or professional evaluation first
