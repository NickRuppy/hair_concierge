# Curl Definition Routine: Assembly Rules

Build the routine from four practical legs:

1. Scalp state drives the wash step.
2. Fibre state drives conditioner, mask, and leave-in direction.
3. Curl pattern and thickness drive sectioning, styling stack, and product weight.
4. Life fit decides how much routine the user can actually repeat.

Scalp and wash routing:
- fast-greasing scalp: cleanse the scalp well; use clarifying or reset logic only when buildup or scalp feel calls for it
- dry, tight, non-medical scalp: use gentler wash choices and avoid aggressive reset stacking
- oily and flaky scalp: consider dandruff or antifungal logic, but keep cadence tied to product directions and scalp context
- inflamed, painful, weeping, crusting, pustular, or patchy-loss scalp: stop routine assembly and redirect
- OWC is a strong default for curlier/coily or definition-seeking users when scalp and porosity allow; use CWC or lighter pre-wash protection for fine, low-porosity, or easily weighed-down hair

Detangling:
- detangle only with conditioner or high slip in the hair
- avoid dry detangling and avoid detangling during shampoo
- work from ends upward in sections with fingers, wide-tooth comb, or flexible detangling brush

Wet styling:
- apply leave-in while hair is fully wet or saturated as the strong default
- use damp application only as an adjustment for fine wavies or users whose pattern is flattened by too much water
- leave-in is default for users seeking definition; choose lighter vehicles for fine hair rather than skipping leave-in
- add a separate styler only when leave-in alone does not hold definition, collapses quickly, or humidity requires more cast
- keep styling products mostly off the roots to avoid flat or greasy-looking canopy

Drying and stack sizing:
- scrunch upward, use sections, and press product in instead of rubbing across curl clumps
- plop or press-dry with t-shirt or microfiber; avoid rough terry-towel drying
- diffuse with low-to-medium heat and airflow, or air-dry if that fits the user
- wait until fully dry before breaking the cast
- fine curls: light leave-in first; add light gel only when needed
- normal curls: leave-in first; add cream or gel by result
- coarse, very curly, or coily hair: leave-in plus one styler is often justified; layer cream and gel only when pattern, porosity, or thickness requires it
- coily hair often trends toward longer wash intervals and richer conditioning, but respect the user's actual cadence
