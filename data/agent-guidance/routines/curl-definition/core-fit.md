# Curl Definition Routine: Core Fit

This framework assembles a complete routine for curly, coily, and definition-seeking wavy users. It supports the natural wave, curl, or coil pattern through wash technique, conditioned detangling, wet styling, controlled drying, and the smallest styling stack that delivers definition.

Good fit:
- curly or coily hair across fine, normal, and coarse thickness
- wavy hair when the user wants visible definition rather than smoother low-maintenance waves
- users with limp, frizzy, uneven, or collapsing pattern who are willing to use wet styling
- returning users whose pattern changed after heat, colour, hormones, postpartum, climate, water, or buildup

Poor fit:
- straight hair or low-definition wavy users who heat-style smooth
- users who will not do any wet styling step
- active medical scalp conditions, painful inflammation, wounds, patchy loss, or persistent flaking that needs treatment first
- severe structural damage where bond-builder or repair stabilization should come before routine assembly

Realistic ceiling:
- more even definition, better clumping, less frizz, and a repeatable wash-day process
- not creating a curl pattern the user does not have, not reversing heat-trained damage, and not replacing medical scalp or hair-loss care
