# Curl Definition Routine: Guardrails

Do not:
- apply this framework to straight or low-definition wavy users who do not want pattern definition
- omit leave-in for users seeking definition; use a lighter leave-in if weight is the issue
- push gel, cream, LOC, LCO, or a full layered stack on every curl user by default
- recommend dry detangling for curly or coily hair
- recommend high-heat blow-drying without a diffuser as curl styling
- treat the wet-strand pull test as a standalone diagnostic
- claim curls contain more protein; the useful framing is bond architecture, geometry, and stress points, not higher protein content
- encode Curly Girl Method rules such as no sulfates, no silicones, or no heat as universal
- treat LOC versus LCO ordering as evidence-backed
- promise to create a curl pattern, reverse heat-trained damage, repair split ends, drive regrowth, or structurally repair hair through routine assembly

Evidence framing:
- wet styling on saturated hair is a strong practitioner default aligned with water and hydrogen-bond chemistry, not a proven rule for every curl user
- OWC is a strong default when scalp and porosity allow, not a universal requirement
- coily wash cadence commonly trends longer, but individual scalp, lifestyle, and user preference still matter
- curl recovery after years of straight-hair handling usually takes weeks to months, not one wash day

Medical redirect:
- stop routine assembly for painful scalp, persistent inflammation, pustules, wounds, weeping, patchy loss, or unexplained heavy shedding
