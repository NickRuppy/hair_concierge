# Straight And Low-Definition Wavy Routine: Assembly Rules

Build the routine from three legs:

1. Scalp state drives the wash step.
2. Fibre state drives conditioner, mask, and leave-in.
3. Hair thickness drives product count and product weight.

Scalp routing:
- fast-greasing scalp without flakes or redness: use a clarifying or stronger cleansing option only when scalp feel calls for it; keep gentle wash options for frequent washing
- dry, tight, mildly itchy, non-medical scalp: prefer gentle or sensitive shampoo and consider soothing pre-shampoo care
- oily and flaky scalp: consider anti-dandruff or antifungal logic, but keep cadence tied to product directions and medical context
- painful, inflamed, weeping, crusting, or patchy-loss scalp: route out of routine assembly

Fibre routing:
- use chemical service, heat use, breakage, mushy wet feel, and straw-like feel before relying on the wet-strand pull test
- over-stretchy or mushy signals: lean repair/protein-aware, but route severe cases to bond-builder guidance first
- snaps-fast or brittle signals: lean moisture and lipid support
- balanced fibre: keep conditioning minimal

Thickness routing:
- fine and balanced: wash plus leave-in can be enough; skip conditioner and mask when they add weight without solving a signal
- fine with fibre signals: wash, conditioner, leave-in; usually skip mask
- normal with fibre signals: wash, conditioner, leave-in; mask only when damage history supports it
- coarse: wash, conditioner, leave-in; mask is more often justified, and cream leave-in may fit better than spray

Meta-rules:
- keep a working routine unchanged unless the user's state changed or buildup is likely
- add the fewest steps needed to solve the problem
- change one product at a time and judge after several wash cycles
- fit the routine to the user's real life, not the ideal version of their bathroom time
