# Straight And Low-Definition Wavy Routine: Guardrails

Do not:
- treat this framework as universal for curly or coily hair
- use the wet-strand pull test as a standalone diagnostic
- recommend a maximum-product routine by default
- choose shampoo from length dryness instead of scalp state
- run two shampoos in sequence as a default
- overuse scalp peel, scrub, or therapy-shampoo combinations
- tell users hair gets used to cosmetic actives and must be switched routinely
- change multiple products at once when troubleshooting
- encode brand picks, exact cadences, or salon-default routines as rules
- promise structural repair, regrowth, length gain, or split-end repair from routine assembly

Evidence framing:
- scalp-state wash matching is stronger than at-home fibre testing
- thickness-based product count is practitioner consensus, not clinical proof
- pull-test outcomes are useful only alongside history and symptoms
- anti-dandruff active rotation is a dermatologist-led exception, not an app-generated routine rule

Medical redirect:
- stop routine assembly for persistent inflammation, painful patches, weeping, scaling, pustules, patchy loss, or unexplained heavy shedding
